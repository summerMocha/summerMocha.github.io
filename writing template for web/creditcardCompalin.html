How to collect data and create graphs in Tableau

This tutorial will teach how to do simple data analyze step by step by spreedsheet and create a simple bar chart with Tableau

January 1, 2016 by Wei Tang

When looking at data regarding complaints made to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau obtained from data.gov, we observed a significant amount of complaints made regarding credit reports, and decided to look at complaints filed against 3 companies in particular; Experian, Equifax, and Transunion.

We chose to look at these companies, as they handle the vast majority of credit reports in the United States, and are increasingly notorious for consistently making mistakes that can cost people loans, apartments, or even jobs.

Recent studies have found that 25% of people have errors in their credit reports, and 5% of people have errors serious enough to cause them to be denied a loan or worse. Mistakes can include having debt on your report that has already been paid or isn’t yours.

People’s identities have been mixed up and never fixed for years. There have even been instances of credit reports indicating a criminal record that doesn’t belong to them.

The first chart shows that the majority of complaints made to the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau towards these companies are about incorrect information on credit reports, or adjacent issues.

The second shows the trend of how many complaints have been filed since October 2012 when the CFPB began taking them. Both indicate that a great number of people in Massachusetts are becoming aware of the potential errors that plague their credit reports.

Whether this is due to rising awareness of the issue, or of the fact that CFPB accepts complaint about them is unclear. But this data doesn’t provide confidence in these credit bureaus’ claims that they are improving their systems.
